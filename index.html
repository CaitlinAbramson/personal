It feels lively and energetic, like I do when talking to friends or listening to music or a good podcast.

Experience

Cierant Corporation

June 2018 - Present

User Experience

As part of the Design Team at Cierant Corporation, I gained experience working with all aspects of the UX lifecycle. I conducted research to learn about users' pain points and created user personas and scenarios to inform our design. During team meetings, we created group sketches, which I then turned into wireframes and prototypes, using Adobe XD and Pop by Marvel. I presented these designs to clients and included them within our specification documents.

With my 2 years at Cierant, I learned the importance of understanding users' needs and designing before development. We have begun the process of shifting from a develop-then-design mentality to one that includes iterative steps of design, development, and stakeholder communication.

Development

I am currently in the process creating an internal Design and Code Library so that it is easier to keep applications stylistically similar and avoid recreating custom solutions. This library will provide snippets of code written in pure HTML/CSS, for Angular and JavaScript frameworks, and in C#. This project will involve modifying and updating code to match modern design standards and our company's branding vision.

I also worked on a front-end project in which I helped set-up, structure, and customize a WordPress site that will be used as marketing and promotional material for one of our applications. Using a Genesis framework with a child theme, plug-ins, and custom HTML and CSS, I established a general structure and workflow for the final site.

Business Analyst

As part of the effort to revolutionize the company's workflow, I redesigned the structure and procedure for writing business and technical requirement documents. The documents include annotated wireframes or prototypes, followed by user stores, and depending on the project, sprint tasks for developers. This document serves as a platform for communication and compromise between the developers and stakeholders. While writing these documents myself, I worked closely with developers to ensure we were promising a minimal viable product that was reasonable and within budget.

Testing

In addition to changing the documentation process, I helped to create a new QA Testing system. I researched typical procedures and designed a SmartSheet and Excel guideline and template. Any future large or full-system testing task began with adapting the testing template to fit the application's architecture. After this template proved to work well, I used it to test applications I helped design.

Social Neuroscience Lab (UVA)

August 2017 - May 2018

Research Assistant

As a research assistant, I ran trials and collected data for two experiments. The first was an EEG study examining the effects of cognitive and emotional regulation techniques. The second experiment studied the effect of a positive, negative, or neutral visual imagery on one's tolerance of an ice-water stressor task.

Being a research assistant helped me to realize my passion for being involved with the application of research. My boss introduced me to the field of User Experience for the first time. It excited me to find a way to combine neurological and psychological research with modern technological advancements.

Product Re-Branding

MetaBall Rebranding

MetaBall Energy Bites is a family-owned product, and in 2017, we decided to rebrand the product. To see the old packaging for comparison, click here. I helped create sketches and design components, and a contracted designer produced a number of packaging options. To help learn which packaging is most attractive to our target population best, we sent out a survey in which potential buyers ranked the designs. We also did in-store experimentation to analyze the designs that stood out on the shelves best. With these results, we created the final packaging which combined elements from a few of the design options.


With the new packaging, we needed our website and social media to match the branding. I contributed to the web design meetings, talking to developers and user experience designers to create a realistic plan for the new site. With each version of the site delivered to us, I helped test for bugs and tweak design elements. For our social media sites, I created a content guideline to make sure our posts are directed towards our intended consumers. I also developed a calendar to plan out posts and timing.

DIY Pack Low-Fidelity Prototype

For this project, we were given an existing style guide, but no UX or user pathways. I received this project 4 hours before it was to be presented on a client call. We needed to demonstrate two separate paths: a fully customized box and a template-based box. This screen is part of the template-based experience. I wanted to create a low-fidelity prototype instead of a wireframe because I wanted to show the client we were taking their branding and aesthetics into consideration; it wasn't just about the functionality.

I had a few constraints on my design. Our developers were implementing XMPie software, and our budget did not allow for much customization of its out-of-the-box UX or UI. By speaking to our developers and understanding our client's requirements, I was able to quickly create this design and meet everyone's needs. The final deliverable was a low-fidelity clickable prototype (using Adobe XD).
